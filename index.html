MEDUSA
Because nobody is born a villain - not even Medusa.

Medusa

My greatest crime, an act of love:
a pact unspoken, souls imbued.
Her temple we sin, drink in his poison,
cast me his Venus, he my sweet ‘Seidon.
Heart once loveless, now pursued.
My greatest crime. . . an act of love?

Bliss short lived, hath hellfire spawn.
Ugly creature I soon become –
My white silk skin turned unsightly
chartreuse,
and golden locks now snakes not to ruse.
Deserve not I, to be treated like scum.
Bliss short lived, hath hellfire spawn.

Curse Athena, sly heart of stone.
“How dare she look at me this way!
I am not one to be taken for defeat.
Be wary my vengeance, ‘gulf thy conceit.
And blast this day I choose not pray.”
Curse Athena, sly heart of stone!

So let me live my life so shrewd,
and wallow in great misery.
For every life I yet glance upon
turns to stone, manifest the fear they don.
This lonely fight I have to lead!

So let me live my life so shrewd.

He too shall rot, oh loveless soul,
to forswear me in times of need –
a marriage rooted in cruel falsities!
Scared away by my mere cosmetology.
Love this soiled is a love best freed.
He too shall rot, oh loveless soul.

As a monster, you treat me.
And so a monster, I shall be.


WHEN LOVE ENDURES
Cupid and Psyche fall in love through sonnet.

When Love Endures

Born last progeny of sovereign's loins,
Worshipped yet loved not, hellfire laden.
Cursed with beauty that hath Venus purloin,
The glum fate of a lovely young maiden.

Impatient rage calls down winged son Cupid,
Who cannot thwart her charms, nor tries to fight.
Thus commences the tale of love undisputed,
Wed and yet barred by the absence of sight.

Under strict orders to stay under kohl,
Not quite enough for this fearless mistress
For curiosity swallows her whole –
A yearning to see, moon bear her witness!

To win back the love of goddess lone heir,
Unto a quest she does boldly embark.
Her suffering he can no longer bear,
They reunite, yet no longer in dark.

The ancient myth of Cupid and Psyche,
When love endures against odds almighty.


FALL OF MAN
Yet how should we live without due cognition?

Fall of Man

lovers flicker flicker under the veneer of night –
a pair of perfection unperfected in dim intuition.
dreamers of desire embrace in moonless moonlight.

curiosity encompasses her, yearning blind sight
(yet how should we live without due cognition?)
lovers flicker flicker under the veneer of night.

and he beseeches, a cynic and a coward forthright,
and pleads tentative warnings of Father’s prohibition
dreamers of desire embrace in moonless moonlight.

enticed with the ad-vice of freedom contrite
to hear sotto voce ‘stead the serpentine
susurration.
dreamers of desire embrace in moonless moonlight.

beyond the reaches of experience, crying how bright
to rue what they lost of their own cruel volition
lovers flicker flicker under the veneer of night.

Father arrives, amassed – a mass of pure height
a disenchanted admonition of prophetic ruination
lovers flicker flicker under the veneer of night.
dreamers of desire embrace in moonless moonlight.
